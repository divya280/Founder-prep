# DPIIT Startup Recognition (Startup India)

## What it is

"DPIIT recognition" and "Startup India registration" refer to the same
certification — an official government recognition, issued by the Department
for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce
and Industry, confirming an entity qualifies as a "startup" under the
government's notification. It is not a substitute for incorporation — a
company, LLP, or partnership must already exist before applying. Applications
are processed through the National Single Window System (nsws.gov.in) and
the Startup India portal.

## Why it matters

Without recognition, a legally incorporated company cannot access:
- Section 80-IAC income tax holiday (3 consecutive years out of the first
  10 years since incorporation)
- Self-certification under a set of labour and environmental laws (reduces
  inspection burden)
- 50% rebate on trademark filing fees, 80% rebate on patent filing fees
- Fast-tracking of patent applications
- Eligibility for the Startup India Seed Fund and Fund of Funds
- Easier winding-up provisions for startups

## Eligibility (all conditions must hold simultaneously)

- **Entity type**: Private Limited Company, Registered Partnership Firm, LLP,
  or Cooperative Society. Sole proprietorships and HUFs are **not** eligible.
- **Age**: Not more than 10 years from date of incorporation (extended
  window for a Deep Tech category — verify current cutoff).
- **Turnover**: Below the prescribed annual turnover ceiling in any financial
  year since incorporation (this ceiling has moved over time — confirm the
  current figure on the portal before applying).
- **Not formed by splitting/reconstructing** an existing business.
- **Innovation/scalability**: Working towards development or improvement of
  a product, process, or service, and/or has a scalable business model with
  high potential for employment/wealth creation.

## Process (high level)

1. Register on the Startup India portal, or create an account on NSWS
   (nsws.gov.in) and add "Registration as a Startup" under Central Approvals.
2. Fill in entity details (incorporation number, registration date, sector,
   category).
3. Upload: Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, and a clear innovation
   write-up describing the problem, solution, technology, and scalability —
   generic language is the most common rejection reason.
4. Optionally attach: pitch deck, funding proof, awards, patent publications,
   support letters from a recognized incubator or government body.
5. Submit — an acknowledgement number is issued immediately. Track status on
   the dashboard.

There is **no government fee** at any stage. Anyone charging a mandatory fee
for the DPIIT certificate itself is not an authorized channel.

## Separate from recognition — 80-IAC tax exemption

DPIIT recognition and the Section 80-IAC tax holiday are two **separate**
applications. After DPIIT recognition, a startup applies separately to the
Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) for 80-IAC. IMB review focuses on
technological innovation, market potential, and employment contribution, and
review timelines have varied — confirm the current turnaround on the portal.

## Common pitfalls

- Treating DPIIT recognition as if it were company incorporation — it is not.
- A vague or templated innovation statement is the single largest cause of
  rejection.
- Assuming DPIIT recognition alone unlocks 80-IAC — it does not; that is a
  separate, additional application.
- Assuming DPIIT recognition changes GST obligations — it does not; GST
  registration rules are independent and apply regardless of startup status.
- Submitting forged or mismatched documents — this carries a penalty framework
  under the scheme's rules and can bar future recognition.

## Penalties / risk of non-compliance

There's no "penalty" for not registering — DPIIT recognition is optional,
not a legal requirement to operate. The risk is purely opportunity cost:
missing the tax holiday window, IPR fee rebates, and self-certification
benefits. However, submitting fraudulent documents to obtain recognition
does carry penalties under the scheme.

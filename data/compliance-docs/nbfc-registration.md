# NBFC Registration

## What it is

A Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) is a company (not an LLP, partnership,
or sole proprietorship) that carries on financial activity — lending,
investment, asset finance, etc. — as its principal business, without holding
a full banking license. NBFCs are regulated by the Reserve Bank of India
under Section 45-IA of the RBI Act, 1934, and **must** obtain a Certificate
of Registration (CoR) before commencing operations.

## When registration is triggered — the "50-50 test"

A company must register as an NBFC with RBI if **both** of the following
hold:
- Financial assets constitute more than 50% of total assets, **and**
- Income from financial assets constitutes more than 50% of gross income.

If a company meets both conditions, operating without RBI registration is
unlawful and can attract regulatory action, penalties, and prosecution.

## Eligibility and capital requirement

- Must be incorporated as a Private Limited or Public Limited Company under
  the Companies Act, 2013 (LLPs, partnerships, and sole proprietorships are
  **not** eligible to become NBFCs).
- The Memorandum of Association's main objects clause must explicitly
  include financial activity (lending, investment, etc.).
- **Minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF)**: sources are split between citing ₹2
  crore and ₹10 crore as the current minimum. The more consistently
  corroborated and more recent figure across sources is **₹10 crore**,
  described as having been raised from an earlier ₹2 crore requirement
  effective October 2022 for new applicants. Given the conflicting figures
  and the direct legal stakes of getting this wrong, **treat ₹10 crore as
  the working assumption but explicitly flag it as needing RBI Master
  Direction verification before this figure is used in any real founder
  guidance.**
  - NOF is broadly: paid-up equity capital + free reserves − accumulated
    losses, deferred revenue expenditure, and intangible assets − certain
    inter-group investments/loans above a threshold.
  - Some sub-categories (e.g., NBFC-MFI, NBFC-Factor, Core Investment
    Companies, Housing Finance Companies) have different NOF minimums under
    separate frameworks — confirm the applicable category-specific figure.
- At least one-third of directors are commonly expected to have substantial
  (often cited as 10+ years) experience in banking/financial services, and
  directors/shareholders must have a clean credit history.

## Process (high level)

1. Incorporate (or already hold) a Private/Public Limited Company with a
   financial-activity objects clause.
2. Infuse and deposit the required NOF as liquid equity capital; obtain a
   statutory auditor's certificate confirming the NOF calculation.
3. Prepare a multi-year business plan (target market, products, financial
   projections, risk management, funding plan).
4. Apply online via the RBI COSMOS portal; then submit physical documents
   to the relevant RBI regional office.
5. RBI reviews (multi-month process; commonly cited as 3–6 months),
   including scrutiny of source of funds, business model, and promoter
   background; may request clarifications or an interview.
6. On approval, RBI issues the Certificate of Registration — lending
   activity cannot commence before the certificate is physically in hand.

## After registration — immediate follow-on obligations

- Registration with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND) for PMLA
  compliance.
- Membership with the major Credit Information Companies (CIBIL, Equifax,
  Experian, CRIF High Mark).
- Registration with the Central KYC Registry (CERSAI).
- Appointment of a statutory auditor and ongoing prudential/return filings
  (e.g., quarterly returns on deposits and prudential norms, asset-liability
  management returns).

## Common pitfalls

- Attempting to register as an LLP or partnership — not eligible under the
  RBI framework.
- Underestimating capital requirements — NOF must be genuine liquid equity,
  not notional or locked in fixed/group-company assets.
- Assuming a fintech that merely facilitates lending (without holding the
  loans on its own books) automatically needs NBFC registration — the
  50-50 test is about the company's own asset/income composition; a pure
  technology-platform model may or may not trigger it depending on
  structure. This is a genuinely fact-specific determination — flag for a
  founder to get direct legal advice rather than relying on a generic
  RAG answer.

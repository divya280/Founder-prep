# MCA / ROC Annual Filing

## What it is

Every company registered under the Companies Act, 2013 (Private Limited,
Public Limited, OPC, Section 8) must file annual returns and financial
statements with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) via the MCA portal, every
financial year — **regardless of turnover, profitability, or whether the
company has done any business at all.** LLPs have an equivalent but
separate annual filing requirement (Form LLP-8 and LLP-11).

## Key forms for a Private Limited Company

- **AOC-4** — Financial statements (balance sheet, P&L, cash flow
  statement, director's report). Must be filed within 30 days of the AGM.
  Certified by a CA and digitally signed.
- **MGT-7 / MGT-7A** — Annual return with shareholder, director, and
  share-capital details. MGT-7A is a simplified version available to Small
  Companies (paid-up capital ≤ a defined ceiling and turnover ≤ a defined
  ceiling — check current figures) and OPCs. Due within 60 days of the AGM.
- **DIR-3 KYC** — Annual KYC for every director holding a DIN. Due annually,
  typically by 30 September.
- **ADT-1** — Notice of auditor appointment/reappointment.

## Timeline logic

All deadlines chain off the Annual General Meeting (AGM):
1. AGM must be held within 6 months of financial year-end (for a
   April–March financial year, that's by 30 September).
2. AOC-4 is due within 30 days of the AGM.
3. MGT-7/MGT-7A is due within 60 days of the AGM.

A company with a zero-transaction year still has to conduct the AGM and
file a "nil" AOC-4 and MGT-7 with current director/shareholder details —
there is no small-turnover exemption from the filing obligation itself
(only from which form variant applies, e.g. MGT-7A instead of MGT-7).

## Penalties for late/missed filing

- **Late filing fee**: commonly cited as ₹100/day per form, with **no upper
  cap** — this compounds daily and can become very large very fast. Confirm
  the current fee schedule, since MCA has periodically run fee-waiver
  windows for specific filing cycles.
- **Statutory fine** (separate from the late fee): under the Companies Act,
  failure to file the annual return can attract a fine on the company and
  personal liability for every officer in default, within a defined range.
- **Strike-off risk**: failing to file annual returns for two or more
  consecutive years can result in the company being struck off the
  Register of Companies. A struck-off company loses legal existence, cannot
  contract, and faces serious difficulty reopening bank accounts or
  transferring assets.
- **Director disqualification**: directors of companies that default on
  filing for three consecutive years can be disqualified from being
  appointed/reappointed as a director of any company for a period.

## Common pitfalls

- Assuming a dormant/zero-revenue company is exempt from filing — it isn't,
  unless it has formally obtained dormant company status under the
  Companies Act.
- Missing the AGM deadline itself, not just the filing deadline — the filing
  clocks (30/60 days) start from the AGM date, so a delayed AGM cascades
  into delayed filings.
- Treating this as a one-time incorporation task — it is an **annual**
  recurring obligation for the life of the company.
- Not budgeting for the CA/CS certification step; AOC-4 and MGT-7 both
  require professional certification/signature, not just self-filing.

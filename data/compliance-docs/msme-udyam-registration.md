# MSME / Udyam Registration

## What it is

Udyam Registration is the official, free, online mechanism (replacing the
older Udyog Aadhaar system since July 2020) for a business to be recognized
as a Micro, Small, or Medium Enterprise (MSME) under the MSMED Act, 2006.
Registration is Aadhaar-based, linked to PAN and GSTIN, and issues a
permanent Udyam Registration Number (URN) with a QR-coded digital
certificate.

## Classification criteria — verify current thresholds before use

Historically (and as of the most recent widely-confirmed revision, effective
1 April 2025), classification used a **composite criterion**: both
investment in plant & machinery/equipment AND annual turnover, with the
enterprise placed in the higher of the two categories if they point to
different bands. Some more recent sources describe a shift toward a
turnover-only classification model — this could not be fully confirmed
against a primary gazette source, so **treat the exact current criteria and
thresholds as unverified and confirm on the Udyam portal or with a CA before
using them for eligibility decisions**.

What is consistently corroborated:
- Both manufacturing and service enterprises are eligible.
- Trading businesses (wholesale/retail/e-commerce reselling) are generally
  **not** eligible for Udyam registration under the MSMED Act as it has
  stood — this has been periodically discussed for change but confirm
  current status.
- Export turnover is excluded from the turnover calculation used for
  classification (an incentive for exporters).
- If an enterprise crosses its category's ceiling, it isn't reclassified
  immediately — a grace period (commonly one additional year) applies
  before it moves to the next category or exits MSME status.

## Why founders register

- Priority Sector Lending access and collateral-free credit guarantees
  (CGTMSE) for eligible micro/small enterprises.
- 50% rebate on trademark government filing fees (same rebate DPIIT
  recognition provides).
- Buyers are legally required to pay registered MSME suppliers within a
  defined period (commonly cited as 45 days); delayed payment can trigger
  interest liability and tax-reporting consequences for the buyer under
  Section 43B(h) of the Income Tax Act.
- Access to government tenders with MSME-reserved quotas, subsidy schemes,
  and (per some 2026 Budget announcements) enhanced credit-guarantee and
  export-support schemes — confirm current scheme availability.

## Process (high level)

1. Go to the Udyam Registration portal.
2. Enter Aadhaar number of the proprietor/authorized signatory; verify via
   OTP.
3. Enter PAN and validate; declare ITR filing status and GSTIN (if
   applicable).
4. Select NIC code(s) for the business activity.
5. Enter investment and turnover figures (portal can auto-fetch some data
   from linked IT/GST records).
6. Submit — certificate issued immediately, no fee, self-declaration based
   (no documents to upload for most applicants).

## Ongoing obligation

Registered enterprises are expected to keep turnover/investment data updated
annually (often auto-updated via linked GST/IT filings). Failing to update
can lead to misclassification or the registration being marked inactive.

## Common pitfalls

- Confusing MSME/Udyam registration with DPIIT/Startup India recognition —
  they are separate registrations with separate (though overlapping)
  benefits; a business can hold both.
- Registering a trading business expecting MSME scheme access it may not be
  eligible for.
- Letting turnover/investment data go stale, risking loss of correct
  classification and associated benefits.

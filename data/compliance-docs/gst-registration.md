# GST Registration

## What it is

GST (Goods and Services Tax) registration gives a business a GSTIN (GST
Identification Number) and brings it into India's unified indirect tax
system. Registration is done online via the GST portal.

## Who must register — turnover thresholds

Aggregate turnover is calculated on a PAN-India basis (all branches under
one PAN combined) and includes taxable, exempt, export, and inter-state
supplies.

- **Goods**: mandatory once aggregate turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh/year in
  most states; ₹20 lakh/year in special category states.
- **Services**: mandatory once aggregate turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh/year in
  most states; ₹10 lakh/year in special category states.
- **Special category states** (lower thresholds) generally include:
  Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura,
  Uttarakhand, and sometimes others — the exact list has shifted over time,
  confirm current classification for the founder's state.

## Mandatory registration regardless of turnover

Certain categories must register even below the threshold:
- E-commerce operators, and sellers supplying through e-commerce platforms
  (liable for TCS)
- Casual taxable persons and non-resident taxable persons
- Businesses making inter-state taxable supply of goods (some small-service
  exceptions apply)
- Persons required to deduct TDS or collect TCS under GST
- Agents/principals making taxable supply on behalf of another
- Persons liable under reverse charge mechanism
- Online service providers supplying from outside India to unregistered
  persons in India; online money gaming operators from outside India
- Input Service Distributors (ISD)

## Voluntary registration

A business below the threshold can register voluntarily — common among
startups that want to claim Input Tax Credit, issue GST-compliant invoices,
or appear more credible to larger B2B clients. Once registered, all normal
GST compliance obligations (returns, invoicing rules, record-keeping) apply
regardless of whether registration was mandatory or voluntary.

## Process (high level)

1. Go to the GST portal, enter PAN, mobile number, and email.
2. Verify via OTP to generate a Temporary Reference Number (TRN).
3. Submit business details and required documents (PAN, proof of business
   address, bank account details, authorized signatory ID/address proof).
4. On verification, a GSTIN and registration certificate are issued
   (statutory timeline is a few working days if there are no queries).

## Composition Scheme (optional, for smaller businesses)

Businesses under a defined turnover ceiling can opt for the Composition
Scheme: simplified quarterly filing and a lower fixed tax rate, but **no**
Input Tax Credit can be claimed. This is a trade-off — beneficial for small
traders with mostly end-consumer sales, less useful for B2B businesses whose
customers want to claim ITC on their purchases.

## Consequences of not registering after crossing the threshold

- Financial penalty: typically 10% of the tax due, or a fixed minimum
  amount, whichever is higher (higher penalties apply for deliberate
  evasion).
- Loss of ability to claim Input Tax Credit for the unregistered period.
- Possible goods seizure/confiscation in cases of intentional evasion.

## Common pitfalls

- Under-counting turnover by excluding exempt supplies — aggregate turnover
  includes exempt and export turnover, not just taxable sales.
- Assuming DPIIT/Startup India recognition changes GST obligations — it
  does not.
- Selling through e-commerce marketplaces without registering, assuming the
  platform's own GST registration covers the seller — it does not.
- Missing the registration deadline after crossing the threshold mid-year —
  registration is required promptly upon crossing, not at year-end.

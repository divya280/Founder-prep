# FSSAI Registration / License

## What it is

Any business handling food in India — manufacturing, processing, storage,
distribution, sale, including cloud kitchens, home-based food sellers, and
online food sellers — must hold an FSSAI credential under the Food Safety
and Standards Act, 2006. There are three tiers, distinguished by annual
turnover and, for some categories, by business activity type regardless of
turnover. Applications are filed on the FoSCoS portal
(foscos.fssai.gov.in).

## Three tiers (thresholds effective 1 April 2026)

| Tier | Turnover band | Issued by |
|---|---|---|
| **Basic Registration** (Form A) | Up to ₹1.5 crore/year | Local/state authority |
| **State License** (Form B) | Above ₹1.5 crore up to ₹50 crore/year | State FSSAI authority |
| **Central License** (Form B) | Above ₹50 crore/year | Central FSSAI authority |

These thresholds were raised substantially from the prior limits (₹12 lakh
and ₹20 crore) by an FSSAI order dated 13 March 2026, implementing
amendment regulations notified 10 March 2026, following a NITI Aayog
recommendation to reduce compliance burden on small food businesses. If
advising on a filing that predates 1 April 2026, use the older thresholds
instead.

## Central License required regardless of turnover

Some activities trigger a mandatory Central License **irrespective of
turnover**:
- Food import or export
- 100% export-oriented units
- Businesses operating in two or more states
- Supply to central government agencies
- Certain multi-state e-commerce food operations, and operations at
  airports/seaports/railway stations

## Process (high level)

1. Create an account on foscos.fssai.gov.in.
2. Select application type (new license/registration, renewal,
   modification).
3. Answer questions about turnover, activity type, and multi-state
   operation — the portal recommends the applicable tier.
4. Complete Form A (Basic) or Form B (State/Central) with business,
   product-category, and premises details.
5. Upload supporting documents (identity/address proof, business
   incorporation proof, food safety management plan for State/Central
   tiers).
6. Pay the applicable fee; for State/Central tiers, a premises inspection
   may occur before approval.
7. Processing time varies by tier — commonly cited as roughly 7–15 working
   days for Basic Registration and 30–90 days for State/Central Licenses,
   depending on inspection scheduling; confirm current portal SLAs.

## Validity

As of the 2026 amendment, FSSAI registrations/licenses have **perpetual
validity** (no fixed expiry) provided the holder maintains compliance and
files required annual returns — this is a change from the older model,
which required periodic renewal (1–5 year validity windows). Confirm
whether perpetual validity applies to the founder's specific
registration/license category, since the source material notes some open
implementation questions remain.

## Category changes

If turnover grows past the current tier's ceiling, the FBO must apply to
upgrade (modification application on FoSCoS) — per the 2026 amendment, this
transition is designed to be automatic/no-cost at the point the top of a
category is reached, retaining the existing license number. If turnover
drops and a lower tier becomes applicable, the FBO can apply to downgrade.

## Penalties

Operating a food business without a valid FSSAI registration/license can
attract a penalty (commonly cited up to ₹5 lakh) and imprisonment up to six
months under the Food Safety and Standards Act — confirm current figures.
Food delivery and marketplace platforms typically also require a valid
FSSAI number during seller onboarding.

## Common pitfalls

- Assuming a home-based or cloud-kitchen food business is exempt — it is
  not; at minimum, Basic Registration is required.
- Not accounting for the multi-state rule — a business with a Central
  License at head-office level may still need separate State
  Licenses/Registrations for individual state units depending on that
  unit's own turnover.
- Missing that FSSAI compliance is location-specific: each premises
  generally needs its own registration/license, not one blanket credential
  for the whole business.
